# National Heart Foundation of Australia: Hypertension Guidelines

This guideline outlines the diagnostic criteria, risk assessment, and treatment pathways for hypertension management in Australian clinical practice.

## Blood Pressure Classifications (Adults)

* **Normal:** Systolic <120 mmHg AND Diastolic <80 mmHg.
* **Normal-High (Pre-hypertension):** Systolic 120–129 mmHg AND/OR Diastolic 80–84 mmHg.
* **Grade 1 Hypertension (Mild):** Systolic 130–139 mmHg AND/OR Diastolic 85–89 mmHg.
* **Grade 2 Hypertension (Moderate–Severe):** Systolic &ge;140 mmHg AND/OR Diastolic &ge;90 mmHg.

## Clinical Management Flow

1. **Lifestyle Interventions:** Recommend for all patients with blood pressure >120/80. Includes sodium reduction (<2g/day), regular aerobic exercise (30 mins/day), alcohol limitation, and smoking cessation.
2. **Pharmacological Therapy:**
   - Initiate immediate drug treatment for patients with Grade 2 Hypertension (&ge;140/90 mmHg).
   - Initiate drug treatment for patients with Grade 1 Hypertension (130-139/85-89 mmHg) who also have high absolute cardiovascular disease (CVD) risk (&ge;15% 10-year risk) or target organ damage.
   - First-line anti-hypertensive drugs: ACE inhibitors (ACEIs), Angiotensin II Receptor Blockers (ARBs), Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs), or Thiazide-like Diuretics.
3. **Special Cohorts:** For diabetic patients, the target blood pressure is strictly <130/80 mmHg. ACE inhibitors or ARBs are prioritized for renal protection.

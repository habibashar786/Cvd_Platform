# National Heart Foundation of Australia: Lipid Management Guidelines

This guideline outlines the diagnostic criteria, risk assessment, and treatment pathways for lipid and cholesterol management.

## Cholesterol Classifications (mmol/L & mg/dL conversion)

* **Total Cholesterol:**
  - Desirable/Normal: <5.0 mmol/L (<193 mg/dL)
  - Borderline High: 5.0–6.1 mmol/L (193–239 mg/dL)
  - High: &ge;6.2 mmol/L (&ge;240 mg/dL)
* **LDL Cholesterol (Primary therapeutic target):**
  - Desirable: <3.0 mmol/L (<116 mg/dL)
  - High Risk Patients Target: <1.8 mmol/L (<70 mg/dL)
  - Very High Risk Patients Target: <1.4 mmol/L (<55 mg/dL)

## Lipid Lowering Therapy

1. **Cardiovascular Risk Categorization:**
   - Always evaluate lipid levels in the context of absolute 10-year CVD risk.
   - High CVD absolute risk (&ge;15%) justifies immediate initiation of lipid-lowering pharmacotherapy regardless of baseline cholesterol levels.
2. **Statins (HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors):**
   - Statins are first-line therapy. Prioritize high-intensity statin therapy (Atorvastatin 40–80mg or Rosuvastatin 20–40mg) for high-risk cohorts.
   - Target is &ge;50% reduction in LDL-C from baseline.
3. **Combination Therapy:**
   - Add Ezetimibe 10mg if LDL-C targets are not achieved on maximally tolerated statin dose.
   - Consider PCSK9 inhibitors for patients with familial hypercholesterolemia or extreme high risk.
